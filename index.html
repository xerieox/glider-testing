The 2026 Albatross Study

New observations in avian gliding suggest that wingtip vortices play a larger role in energy conservation than previously modeled. By adjusting the dihedral angle of synthetic composite wings, we achieved a 12% increase in overall glide ratio under standard atmospheric conditions.

Experimental data collected from the Dublin test site confirms that thermal lift is maximized when surface temperature differentials exceed 4.5 degrees Celsius. Cross-referencing with satellite wind data has enabled a new predictive model that outperforms prior benchmarks by a statistically significant margin.

Composite Wing Flex Analysis — Interim Report

Phase II testing of our carbon-fibre laminate series concluded in March 2026. High-speed camera arrays captured micro-deformation events at 4,000 fps, revealing previously undetected flutter signatures near the wingtip at high angles of attack. The findings have implications for both manned and unmanned glider certification standards.

Collaborators at the Dublin Institute of Technology have begun integrating our flex data into their finite element models, with joint publication expected in Q3 2026.

Atmospheric Boundary Layer Interactions

Our meteorological subteam has published updated coefficients for drag estimation within the planetary boundary layer, improving accuracy for low-altitude soaring flight by approximately 8.3% over the ISO 2533 standard atmosphere model. Current Research Focus Areas

Six active research tracks across aerodynamics, materials, and computational modelling.

Aerodynamics

Nonlinear Lift Curve Modelling at High Incidence Angles

Extending classical thin-airfoil theory to account for leading-edge separation bubbles observed above 14° AoA in low-Reynolds-number gliders.

Materials

Bio-Inspired Morphing Wing Structures

Investigating shape-memory polymer actuators embedded in wing skins to achieve continuous camber adaptation during flight without mechanical linkages.

Computational

LES Simulation of Turbulent Wake Interactions

Large-eddy simulations on GPU clusters to characterise wake hazard zones for formation gliding and ridge-soaring in complex terrain.

Meteorology

Convective Thermal Forecasting for Cross-Country Soaring

Machine learning models trained on radiosonde data to predict thermal strength and spacing with sub-10km spatial resolution up to 6 hours ahead.

Structures

Fatigue Life Estimation for CFRP Spars Under Gust Loading

Developing a probabilistic fatigue framework that accounts for the non-Gaussian gust statistics encountered in mountain wave soaring conditions.

Flight Dynamics

Pilot-Induced Oscillation Suppression via Passive Damping

Evaluating passive viscoelastic damper configurations in control linkages to reduce susceptibility to PIO in high-performance two-seat trainers.
